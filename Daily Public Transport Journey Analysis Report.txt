Public Transport Ridership Analysis & Forecasting Report

Overview

This documentation summarizes the multi-year analysis of daily public transport passenger journeys and presents a 7-day forecast for multiple service types. The study focuses on understanding ridership patterns, identifying operational challenges, and generating data-driven predictions using Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA).

Dataset Summary

The dataset contains daily ridership counts for the following service categories:

Local Route

Rapid Route

Peak Service

Light Rail

School Service

Each entry includes:
Date, Route Type, Daily Passenger Count, and Operational Notes (e.g., holiday effects).

Time span: Multiple years of continuous daily observations, allowing strong seasonal and trend-based modeling.

Observed Long-Term Trends

Local and Rapid Routes show a gradual upward trend, indicating increasing urban mobility demand.

Light Rail maintains a stable long-term pattern but occasionally fluctuates due to infrastructure upgrades and city events.

Peak Service remains comparatively steady, serving mostly fixed-time commuters.

School Service exhibits a repetitive term-based seasonal structure.

High Volatility in Small Services

Small-volume categories such as Peak Service and School Routes experience sharp day-to-day fluctuations.
Factors contributing to volatility:

Exam weeks

Unexpected holidays

Event-based traffic changes

Weather-driven travel behavior shifts

These fluctuations require smoothing techniques before forecasting.

Distribution Characteristics

Ridership values display a right-skewed distribution, with most days having moderate demand but a few extreme high-volume days.
This behavior is common in public transport systems, especially during:

Festivals

Public gatherings

Long weekends

Tourist seasons

Impact of Special Events

Special events cause sudden spikes or dips in ridership. Examples include:

City festivals

Government holidays

Sporting events

Public strikes

Heavy rainfall or weather alerts

These irregularities create outliers in the time series and require careful preprocessing.
